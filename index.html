Lumbini

Lumbini is a significant pilgrimage site located in Rupandehi District, Lumbini Zone of Nepal. It is renowned as the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, who later became known as Buddha. According to Buddhist tradition, Queen Mayadevi gave birth to Siddhartha under a sal tree in the gardens of Lumbini in approximately 563 BCE. The site attracts pilgrims and visitors from around the world and features various monasteries, a sacred pond, and the Ashoka Pillar, erected by the Indian Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE. Lumbini holds immense cultural and spiritual importance, embodying the origins of Buddhism.
